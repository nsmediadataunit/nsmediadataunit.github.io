Systemic inequalities project

section 1

The George Floyd protest brought attention to the systemic discrimination Black people still face in the US.

The protests expanded globally, reaching London, Birmingham, Liverpool, Manchester and Newcastle upon Tyne.

Britain has its own issues. From birth to death, Black people in the UK are still at a disadvantage.

section 2

According to the 2011 census, Black people make up 3.3% of the population in England and Wales, up from just 1% in 2001. By contrast, white people make up 86% of the population.

section 2'

Despite that of the population, the Black population may still be continuing to grow. Just 71.3% of the children born in England and Wales in 2018 were white. Black children accounted for some 4.2% of births.

section 3

According to an MBRRACE-UK report, Black women in the UK have a higher chance of dying in pregnancy with 40 deaths for 100,000 women, five times the rate for white women.

Despite representing 4.2% of the live births, Black infants also represented 7.6% of deaths in England and Wales in 2017.

section 4

While children aged 4-5 of all backgrounds met expected development standards at similar rates, Black children generally receive worse grades as time goes on.

39% of Black children received a strong pass (grade 5 or above) in English and maths compared to 50% of Asian children and 43% of white children.

section 5

Only 5.5% of Black pupils achieved three As or better at A level, compared to around 11% for white, Asian and mixed background pupils.

section 6

Perhaps due to Black Brits living in predominantly urban environments, Black pupils are increasingly more likely to go to university, with almost half (44.5%) entering one in 2019 compared to less than a third of white pupils (30.3%).

section 7

However, Black students generally receive worse grades. 57% of Black students graduated with first and upper second class honours compared to 77% of white students

section 8

The divide continues on in the workforce. Being Black means you’ll earn £1 per hour less than an Asian or white worker, and the pay gap has been growing.

section 9
